# BaytyAI — Voice and Tone

How BaytyAI writes, in every language and every channel. The voice is constant; the tone shifts
by context. If a sentence could appear in any SaaS company's marketing, rewrite it.

---

## Voice principles

1. **Say the specific thing.** "Approvals stall in inboxes for days" beats "we streamline
   collaboration." Concrete nouns, real numbers, named consequences.
2. **Earned authority, no hype.** State what is true plainly. Confidence comes from precision,
   not adjectives.
3. **Short sentences.** One idea each. Cut every word that does not earn its place.
4. **Respect the reader's seniority and intelligence.** Especially government and sovereign
   readers — explain without condescending.
5. **Lead with the strongest line.** Never bury the point under a wind-up.

## Tone by context (register matrix)

| Context | Tone | Example opening |
|---|---|---|
| Government / sovereign | Formal, vision-aligned, relationship-first | "The Gulf is delivering the world's most ambitious built environment." |
| Enterprise developer | Direct, outcome-led | "See where every approval is stuck — before it becomes a delay." |
| Product UI | Plain, instructive, calm | "Three approvals are waiting on you." |
| Thought leadership | Analytical, opinionated, sourced | "Construction's productivity gap is not a technology problem. It is a trust problem." |
| Error / sensitive states | Honest, human, non-blaming | "We couldn't reach the server. Your work is saved." |

## Banned language

Never use: revolutionary, leverage, synergy, next-generation, cutting-edge, world-class,
game-changing, best-in-class, seamless, robust, empower, unlock (figurative), supercharge,
disrupt. No emoji. No exclamation marks. No US-startup hype cadence.

## Numbers and claims

- Every claim has a source, a metric, or is removed.
- No traction, partnership, customer, or compliance claim beyond what is verified. When a figure
  is needed and unconfirmed, write `[INSERT: …]` rather than inventing.
- Never imply government endorsement or Sharia compliance that has not been confirmed.

## Bilingual rules (Arabic is not a translation layer)

- **Transcreate, don't translate.** Arabic copy is written for Arabic readers and GCC business
  culture, not run through a translator. Idioms, honorifics, and rhythm differ.
- **Formal register in Arabic (MSA)** for all official and government-facing copy.
- **Numerals follow locale.** Western Arabic numerals or Eastern Arabic numerals per the chosen
  locale convention — set once, applied consistently. `[INSERT: confirm numeral convention]`
- **Direction is meaning.** RTL is the native reading order for Arabic, not a mirror trick.
  Layouts are designed to feel native in both directions (logical CSS properties, not flips).
- **Names and product terms** stay as BaytyAI; feature names get an agreed Arabic equivalent in
  the lexicon (Phase 2), used consistently.

## Microcopy standards

- Buttons: verb-first, specific. "Request access", not "Submit". "Send reminder", not "OK".
- Empty states: state what will appear and the one action to create it.
- Errors: what happened, whether their data is safe, the next step. No blame, no jargon.
- Confirmations: brief, concrete, and tell them what happens next.

## A short style reference

- Sentence case for headings and buttons. Not Title Case, not ALL CAPS (except the tracked
  wordmark and small mono labels).
- Oxford comma on.
- One space after a full stop.
- Spell out the region as "the Gulf" or "the GCC"; use the full country name on first mention.

## The test
Read it aloud. If it sounds like a brochure, it is wrong. If it sounds like a serious person
explaining something true to a respected peer, it is right.
