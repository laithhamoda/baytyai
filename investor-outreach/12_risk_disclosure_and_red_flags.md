# BaytyAI — Risk Disclosure & Red Flags (INTERNAL ONLY — never show investors)

A pre-mortem. These are the weaknesses a sharp investor will find. Knowing them, and having a
calm, honest framing ready, is the difference between control and panic in the room. Do not
hand this document to anyone outside the company.

---

### 1. No verified traction
**Risk:** No customers, pilots, LOIs, revenue, or ARR in any source. This is the biggest gap
for Series A/B.
**Framing:** Be first to say it. "We're pre-traction by design — here's the design-partner
pipeline `[INSERT]` and the proof-of-demand from `[INSERT: discovery]`." Then sell founder–market
fit and the wedge. Never imply traction you don't have.

### 2. Pre-product company, post-product narrative
**Risk:** The 10-stage roadmap can read as overpromising when the MVP is six features in build.
**Framing:** Separate clearly: "Shipped: X. In build: the MVP. Everything else is the
expansion thesis, not a claim." Investors respect a founder who draws that line themselves.

### 3. Founder track record unestablished (in these materials)
**Risk:** Laith's credibility isn't documented; solo-founder risk.
**Framing:** Lead with the specific, true origin story and regional access. Name the first key
hires and why they'll join. Show you know your gaps.

### 4. "Procore could build this" / thin day-one moat
**Risk:** The durable moat (data, government trust) doesn't exist yet.
**Framing:** Concede the day-one moat is thin, then explain what compounds: regional position,
references, lifecycle data. Honesty here builds more trust than bravado.

### 5. Government sales are slow and concentrated
**Risk:** Long cycles; dependence on a few buyers; political/budget shifts.
**Framing:** Pilot-first to shorten cycles; private developers alongside government; FM revenue
that isn't tied to new project approvals; multi-market spread.

### 6. Pricing and brand inconsistency
**Risk:** AED vs USD pricing, Bayty vs BaytyAI, bayty.com vs baytyai.com across materials. Looks
unfinished; diligence will catch it.
**Framing:** Fix before outreach. There is no good investor-facing answer to "why do your own
docs disagree?"

### 7. No financial model or use of funds
**Risk:** Can't answer burn, runway, ARR targets, or unit economics.
**Framing:** Build a simple, defensible 3-year model before meetings. "Directional, assumptions
stated" beats "we haven't modelled it."

### 8. Compliance/security claims outrunning reality
**Risk:** PDPL/GDPR alignment, "government-ready", and Sharia-compliance language can imply
certifications you don't hold.
**Framing:** Say "aligned to / on a roadmap toward", never "certified/endorsed". Overclaiming
here is the fastest way to lose a sovereign or government buyer.

### 9. Geopolitical and single-region concentration
**Risk:** GCC-only exposure; regional instability; foreign-founder/entity constraints.
**Framing:** Multi-market within GCC; local entity and partners; a credible (later) path beyond
the region. Acknowledge it rather than dodge.

### 10. Execution/operational state (the live-site incident)
**Risk:** Recent build/deploy and filesystem issues, a temporary Safe Browsing flag, and an
unconnected auth/form backend signal an early operation.
**Framing:** Normal for the stage; show the fixes shipped and the discipline (audits, security
headers, consent compliance). Don't volunteer it, but have a composed answer if asked about
product maturity.

---
**Standing rule:** in every meeting, you may concede any weakness honestly — that builds trust.
You may never invent a strength. Diligence finds the lie, and one lie ends the raise.
