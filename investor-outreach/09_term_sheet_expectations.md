# BaytyAI — Term Sheet Expectations (founder-friendly)

Plain-English guide to what to accept, negotiate, and refuse. Not legal advice — engage a
startup-experienced lawyer before signing anything. `[INSERT: …]` where a real figure applies.

## Valuation and structure
- **Pre/post-money:** Know the difference. A "$`[INSERT]` post-money SAFE/round" sets your
  dilution. Negotiate on post-money, not just the headline number.
- **Instrument:** At pre-seed/seed, a post-money SAFE or priced round is normal. Series A is
  almost always a priced equity round.
- **Valuation framing:** Anchor to milestones and comparables, not ego. Over-pricing now creates
  a painful down-round later.

## Option pool
- Expect investors to want a 10–15% pool, often carved **pre-money** (you bear the dilution).
  Negotiate the size down to what your real 18-month hiring plan needs; an oversized pool is
  hidden dilution.

## Economic terms
- **Liquidation preference:** Accept **1x non-participating**. Refuse participating preferred
  ("double dip") and anything above 1x — these quietly take your upside.
- **Anti-dilution:** Accept **broad-based weighted-average**. Refuse **full-ratchet** (punitive
  in a down-round).
- **Dividends:** Avoid cumulative dividends at this stage.

## Control terms
- **Board:** At seed, keep founder control (e.g., 2 founders + 1 investor, or founder majority).
  At Series A, a 2-1-2 or 2-2-1 with an independent is common. Refuse giving up board control
  before you have to.
- **Protective provisions:** Standard (major spend, new shares, sale of company). Keep the list
  tight; refuse vetoes over ordinary operating decisions.
- **Information rights:** Standard quarterly reporting is fine. Cap the level of detail and
  frequency so it doesn't consume the team.
- **ROFR / co-sale / drag-along:** Standard and acceptable. On drag-along, ensure it requires a
  board + majority-preferred threshold, not a single investor.

## GCC sovereign / family-office special considerations
- **Sharia compliance:** Some investors require Sharia-compliant structures (no interest-bearing
  instruments; equity over debt). Be open to it; do not *claim* Sharia compliance until counsel
  confirms the structure. `[INSERT: confirm]`
- **Non-dilutive co-investment vehicles:** Sovereign and government-linked capital may offer
  grants, co-investment, or matching vehicles alongside equity. These can extend runway without
  dilution — welcome them, but read strings on IP, data residency, and exclusivity.
- **Strategic / government-aligned clauses:** Watch for local-hosting, data-sovereignty,
  national-priority, or local-entity requirements. Some are reasonable and even helpful for GTM;
  refuse anything that grants exclusivity, blocks other markets, or constrains a future exit.
- **Pace and relationship:** GCC processes are slower and relationship-driven. Don't force US
  timelines onto them. Keep terms consistent across investors — inconsistency is noticed.
- **Most-favoured-nation / side letters:** Sovereigns may request MFN or special rights. Grant
  narrowly and track every side letter; they compound across rounds.

## What to refuse outright
- Participating preferred or >1x liquidation preference.
- Full-ratchet anti-dilution.
- Loss of board control at seed.
- Exclusivity that blocks markets or future acquirers.
- Unverifiable compliance/endorsement representations you'd have to make.

## Before you sign
- Model your post-round cap table and your dilution through the *next* round.
- Have counsel review every side letter.
- Confirm the lead's references with other founders they've backed.
