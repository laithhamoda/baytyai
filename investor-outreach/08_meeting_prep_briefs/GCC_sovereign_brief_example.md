### Their thesis (one sentence)
Deploys capital aligned with national transformation goals; values local capability building,
long-term partnership, and strategic fit over quick returns.

### Recent relevant areas
- Digital infrastructure and national-platform plays.
- Regional software with government applicability.

### Director background
Senior, well-networked, reads carefully and consults advisors. Judges the founder's character
and seriousness as much as the metrics.

### Likely objections (and answer)
1. "You are early — why now and why you?" → Vision-aligned purpose; deliberate pilot-first
   approach; founder's regional credibility. Patience framed as discipline.
2. "How does this serve national objectives?" → Transparency and auditability in capital
   projects; Arabic-first; local capability and data kept in-region `[INSERT: confirm posture]`.
3. "Who else is involved?" → Be truthful; never imply commitments that don't exist.

### Three talking points to land
1. Built for the region's standards and language — not a foreign tool retrofitted.
2. Pilot-first, low-risk, measurable outcomes, working with local partners.
3. Long-term vision: from project delivery to asset and smart-city operations.

### One thing NOT to say
Do not claim government endorsement, Sharia compliance, or any commitment that isn't verified.
Do not rush to the ask; let the relationship lead.

### The ask for this meeting
Permission to run a scoped pilot conversation and to present to their advisors — not a cheque.

### After the meeting
- Send a brief, respectful follow-up and the overview, in Arabic and English.
- Expect a longer timeline; do not chase. Next touch: 2–3 weeks, with new substance.
