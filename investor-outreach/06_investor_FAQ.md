**2. Is GCC construction software big enough to build a venture outcome?**

**3. Why won't a downturn in GCC megaprojects kill you?**

**4. Why hasn't this been built already?**

auditability became a procurement requirement.

**6. Is the AI real or a wrapper?**
Day one, AI does the unglamorous, high-ROI work: summarising correspondence, extracting data
from drawings and contracts, scoring approval and schedule risk. The defensible AI comes later,
trained on accumulated regional project data. We don't overclaim autonomous AI today.

**7. Why will busy site teams actually adopt it?**
The wedge solves a pain they feel daily — chasing approvals and the latest document version.
Value shows in days, not quarters. Bilingual and mobile-first for GCC field reality. Adoption
needs no rip-and-replace.

**8. What's your data quality problem?**
Real and acknowledged. Early intelligence is rule-based plus lightweight AI; predictive models
come only once data volume and quality justify them. We don't ship predictions we can't stand
behind.

## Defensibility

**9. Procore could build this in six months — why not?**
They can build features, not position. In six months they cannot acquire GCC government
references, Arabic-first regional workflows, lifecycle data from local assets, or public-sector
implementation trust. The moat is regional position plus accumulated data, which a global
English-first incumbent is structurally slow to take.

**10. What is the actual moat on day one?**
Honestly, thin — like every early company. Day-one edge is GCC-native design, founder access,
and focus. The durable moat (government trust, lifecycle lock-in, data) is built over quarters.
We're clear-eyed about that.

**11. What stops a local competitor copying you?**
Speed, product depth, and the compounding data lead. First to become a public authority's
system of record wins switching costs that make replacement painful.

**12. Is the data moat real or a story?**
It becomes real with scale. We state it as a thesis, not a present fact. Today it's a roadmap;
the milestone is the first dataset large enough to produce predictions that beat heuristics.
